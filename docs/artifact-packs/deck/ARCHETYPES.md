# Deck Pack — Archetype Catalog (Phase 2)

The domain knowledge that drives `guidance(archetypeId)`. Each archetype defines the arc,
the slide count, the tone, and the failure modes to steer away from.

## Deck-wide rules (all archetypes)
- **Assertion titles.** Every content-slide title is the *takeaway* ("Enterprise drove 40% of new ARR"), never a topic label ("Revenue").
- **One idea per slide.** ≤ ~40 words of body; the slide carries the headline, the **speaker notes** carry the detail.
- **Structure for length.** Decks > ~10 slides use section dividers.
- **Grounded.** No invented numbers stated as fact — attribute, or frame as illustrative.

---

## Board / Exec Update
**Audience:** leadership / board. **Purpose:** report progress vs. plan, surface decisions.
**Tone:** dense, factual, confident, no fluff. **Length:** ~10–15 slides.

**Arc:**
1. Cover
2. TL;DR / headlines (3–5 assertions)
3. KPIs vs. plan (with prior-period or target comparison)
4. Progress by workstream
5. Risks & issues (honest, with mitigation)
6. Financials / resourcing *(optional)*
7. **The ask / decisions needed** (explicit)
8. Next steps + owners

**Failure modes to avoid:** topic-label titles; metrics with no comparison/context; no clear
ask; wall-of-text; burying bad news.

---

## Pitch / Proposal
**Audience:** a prospect (external MaaS sale) or a funder (internal initiative).
**Purpose:** persuade. **Tone:** punchy, narrative, value-first. **Length:** ~10–12 slides.

**Arc (SCR / problem-first):**
1. Cover
2. The problem / why-now
3. The solution (what it is, in one line)
4. How it works (3 steps or a simple diagram-in-words)
5. Proof (traction, case, or evidence)
6. Differentiation (why us / the sovereignty wall for MaaS)
7. Business model / pricing *(sales)* or cost & plan *(internal)*
8. **The ask / CTA** (specific and time-bound)

**Failure modes to avoid:** leading with product instead of problem; feature-listing instead
of value; no concrete CTA; over-claiming without proof.
