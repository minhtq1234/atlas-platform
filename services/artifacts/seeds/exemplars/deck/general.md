# Business Deck — General (GOLD EXEMPLAR)

Reference for the structure, depth, and tone of a strong **business slide deck** when no
specific archetype applies. Fictional content — do NOT copy it. The craft to match:
every content-slide **title is an assertion** (the takeaway, not a topic label like
"Overview"), each slide holds **one idea** in ≤ ~40 words, and presenter detail lives in
the **speaker notes**. Open with a cover, close with a clear next step.

---

## Slide 1 — Cover
**Title:** Northwind Cloud — Platform Overview
**Subtitle:** How regulated teams ship AI without moving their data
**Notes (presenter):** Set the frame in one line: who it's for and the single promise.

## Slide 2
**Title:** Regulated teams can't use cloud AI without breaking data-residency rules
**Notes:** Start from the audience's problem, stated as a fact they already feel — not from your product.

## Slide 3
**Title:** Northwind runs the models inside your environment, so data never leaves
**Notes:** One-line solution. The "what," not yet the "how."

## Slide 4
**Title:** Three capabilities cover the common cases: deploy, call, govern
- Deploy any catalog model into your VPC
- Call one API; scaling is handled
- Govern access and audit centrally
**Notes:** Group capabilities into a memorable set of three, each a phrase not a paragraph.

## Slide 5
**Title:** Early deployments hold 99.98% uptime with zero data egress
**Notes:** Prove the claim with one or two hard numbers, framed as illustrative if not audited.

## Slide 6
**Title:** Next step: a scoped pilot in your environment within two weeks
**Notes:** Always close on a concrete, low-friction next action with a timeframe.
